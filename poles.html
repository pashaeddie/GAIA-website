Poles Conservation

The polar regions are critical to the Earth's climate system and biodiversity. GAIA is dedicated to protecting these fragile ecosystems through advocacy, research, and conservation initiatives. Join us in our efforts to safeguard the poles for future generations.

Take Action Join Us

Threats to Our Poles

The polar regions face numerous threats, these include climate change, melting ice caps, pollution, and habitat loss.

Ocean Acidification

The increasing levels of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere are causing the polar oceans to become more acidic, which negatively impacts marine life, including shell-forming organisms.

Melting Ice and Sea-Level Rise

The warming climate is causing polar ice caps to melt at an alarming rate, leading to rising sea levels and loss of habitat for polar species.

Climate Change

Rising global temperatures are causing polar ice caps to melt at an alarming rate, leading to rising sea levels and loss of habitat for polar species.

Habitat Loss

Human activities such as oil and gas exploration, shipping, and tourism are disrupting polar ecosystems, leading to habitat degradation and loss for native species.

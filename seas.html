Seas Conservation


Seas are vital ecosystems that support a diverse range of marine life and provide livelihoods for millions of people worldwide. GAIA is dedicated to protecting seas through initiatives such as marine conservation, pollution reduction, and sustainable fishing practices. Join us in our mission to preserve the health and beauty of our seas for future generations.

Threats to Seas

Seas face numerous threats, these include pollution, overfishing, climate change, and habitat destruction.

Pollution

The introduction of harmful substances such as plastics, chemicals, and oil into the seas leads to water contamination and poses a significant threat to marine life.

Overfishing

The excessive harvesting of fish and other marine species disrupts the balance of marine ecosystems, leading to population declines and loss of biodiversity.

Climate Change

Rising sea temperatures and ocean acidification due to increased carbon dioxide levels negatively impact marine ecosystems, affecting coral reefs, fish populations, and overall ocean health.

Habitat Destruction

Coastal development, dredging, and destructive fishing practices lead to the loss of critical marine habitats such as coral reefs, mangroves, and seagrass beds.

Forest Conservation


Forests are essential for maintaining biodiversity, regulating climate, and providing livelihoods for millions of people. GAIA is dedicated to conserving forests through reforestation initiatives, sustainable management practices, and advocacy for forest protection policies. Together, we can ensure that forests continue to thrive for generations to come.

Threats to Forests

Forests face numerous threats, these include deforestation, climate change, illegal logging, and habitat fragmentation.

Deforestation

The clearing of forests for agriculture, logging, and infrastructure development leads to habitat loss and soil erosion in forested areas.

Climate Change

Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns disrupt forest ecosystems, affecting species distribution and increasing vulnerability to pests and diseases.

Illegal Logging

Unsanctioned logging activities contribute to forest degradation, loss of biodiversity, and undermine sustainable forest management efforts.

Habitat Fragmentation

The division of large, contiguous forest areas into smaller, isolated patches negatively impacts wildlife populations and disrupts ecological processes.

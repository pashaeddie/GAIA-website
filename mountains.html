Mountains Conservation


Mountains are vital ecosystems that support diverse flora and fauna. GAIA is committed to protecting these natural wonders through conservation efforts, reforestation projects, and raising awareness about the importance of mountain habitats. Join us in our mission to preserve mountains for future generations.

Threats to Mountains

Mountains face numerous threats, these include deforestation, climate change, mining activities and human encroachment.

Deforestation

The clearing of forests for agriculture, logging, and infrastructure development leads to habitat loss and soil erosion in mountain areas.

Climate Change

Rising temperatures and changing precipitation patterns threaten mountain ecosystems, affecting species distribution and water resources.

Human Encroachment

Expanding human settlements and tourism can lead to pollution, habitat fragmentation, and increased pressure on natural resources in mountain regions.

Mining Activities

Mining operations can lead to land degradation, water contamination, and loss of biodiversity in mountainous areas.
